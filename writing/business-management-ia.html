Executive Summary

The analysis evaluates whether launching/expanding a division is financially justified under realistic demand and cost assumptions. We smooth historical volumes with a four-point moving average, build a PMPM view to normalise revenue and cost per member, and assemble a divisional P&L. Finally, we compute break-even and stress-test it across price, volume, and variable-cost shocks to assess the margin of safety.

Decision Questions

  • What is the most reasonable near-term volume forecast given recent volatility?
  • At current pricing and cost structure, what is the break-even volume and time to break even?
  • How sensitive is profit to ±Δ in price, conversion/volume, and variable cost?
  • What capacity and cash headroom are required for a conservative ramp?

Data & Assumptions

Inputs

  • Historical monthly demand (last 12–18 months).
  • Price per unit and expected discount rate.
  • Variable cost per unit (materials, fulfilment, payment fees).
  • Fixed costs (labour, rent, tooling, marketing baseline).

Assumption hygiene

  • Document each input’s source and rationale.
  • Use ranges/triangles where uncertainty is high; central case = mid-point.
  • Separate one-off launch costs from steady-state fixed costs.

Methods

Four-Point Moving Average

Smooths monthly demand to reduce noise from seasonality and outliers. Used as the baseline for near-term volume planning and staffing.

PMPM (Unit Economics)

Normalises revenue and cost per member/month to compare cohorts and pricing tiers; clarifies contribution margin and CAC payback.

Divisional P&L

Revenue − variable costs = contribution; subtract fixed costs to obtain operating profit. Tracks run-rate vs. plan.
