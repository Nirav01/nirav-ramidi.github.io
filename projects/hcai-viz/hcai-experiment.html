HCAI Empirical Study

Designed and ran a controlled experiment comparing a baseline AI UI against an explainable, human-centred design. Measured task success, time-on-task, perceived trust, usability (SUS), and workload (NASA-TLX).

  • Within-subjects
  • Counter-balanced
  • SUS · TLX · Trust
  • Qual + Quant

Research Questions

  • Does an explainable, human-centred UI improve task success and reduce time-on-task vs a baseline UI?
  • Does it increase perceived trust and usability (SUS) while lowering workload (NASA-TLX)?

Study Design

Design

  • Within-subjects with counter-balanced order (AB/BA) to minimise learning effects.
  • Two conditions: Baseline vs XAI (salient explanations, confidence, data provenance, and recourse).

Variables

  • IV: Interface type (Baseline vs XAI).
  • DV: task success, time-on-task, error rate, trust, SUS, NASA-TLX; plus open-ended feedback.

Participants & Tasks

  • Convenience sample of university participants (mixed backgrounds).
  • Scenario-based decisions (e.g., classify, triage, or recommend) with ground truth available to compute accuracy.
  • Think-aloud encouraged; post-task questionnaires per condition.

Instrumentation & Data

  • Client-side logger captured timestamps, actions, and errors (pseudo-anonymous IDs).
  • Questionnaires: SUS, NASA-TLX, and a Likert trust scale.
  • Post-study semi-structured interview for qualitative themes.

Analysis

  • Checked normality; used paired t-tests or Wilcoxon where appropriate.
  • Effect sizes: dz / r; confidence intervals via bootstrap for robustness.
  • Thematic coding on interview notes; triangulated with quantitative trends.

Findings (Summary)

  • XAI UI improved perceived trust and usability without increasing cognitive load.
  • Participants relied on confidence + rationale to resolve edge cases and reduced unnecessary overrides.
  • Qualitative feedback: participants valued recourse (“what can I do?”) as much as explanations.

Ethics & Limitations

Ethics

  • Informed consent, withdrawal at any time, and data minimisation.
  • No personally identifiable information collected; logs pseudonymised.

Limitations

  • Convenience sampling and short sessions limit ecological validity.
  • Prototype fidelity may cap external validity; follow-up field study recommended.
