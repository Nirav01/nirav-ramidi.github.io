InfoVis Dashboard (Vega-Lite)

An interactive, multi-view dashboard using Vega-Lite with brushing & linking, cross-filters, and tidy data transforms (fold, aggregate, calculate). Designed for fast, explainable exploration without custom JavaScript.

  • Brushing & linking
  • Small multiples
  • Selections & conditions
  • Tidy transforms

Goals

  • Provide an overview → zoom & filter → details flow with clear affordances.
  • Use expressive encodings (position/length/color) and avoid misleading scales.
  • Stay within Vega-Lite primitives—no bespoke JS logic for fairness/grading.

Views & Interactions

Scatter (overview)

Encodes two continuous metrics with color by category and point size by magnitude. A brush selection filters the other views. Tooltips expose exact values.

Bar (composition)

Aggregated counts/means by category; responds to the scatter brush. Conditional color highlights selected categories; labels show percentages.

Line (trend)

Small multiples per category with shared y-scale (can switch to resolve: independent). Hover selection reveals focus lines and value annotations.

Data & Transforms

Tidy transforms

  • fold wide columns to long format for small multiples.
  • calculate derived ratios (e.g., share, normalised scores).
  • aggregate + bin for histograms; window for ranks.

Selections

  • Interval selection on scatter: selection: {brush: {type: 'interval'}}.
  • Single/multi selections to toggle categories with condition color/opacity.
  • transform: [{filter: {selection: 'brush'}}] links views.

Design Decisions

  • Used aligned axes and consistent scales to support visual comparison.
  • Kept color palette accessible (contrast-checked); relied on position/length first.
  • Added legends and helper text (“Drag to filter”) to improve learnability.
  • Reduced clutter with gridlines and minimalist annotations.

Outcome

  • Users could segment patterns quickly via the brush; bars/lines updated without lag.
  • Small multiples clarified category trends; independent scales helped when ranges differed widely.
  • Achieved assignment rubric goals for interaction, correctness, and readability.
